TYPE 2 DIABETES MANAGEMENT GUIDELINES
Clinical Practice Summary (Demo Document — Not Medical Advice)

DIAGNOSIS
Diabetes is diagnosed by any of the following criteria (confirmed on repeat testing):
- Fasting plasma glucose ≥126 mg/dL (no caloric intake for ≥8 hours)
- 2-hour plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL during OGTT (75g oral glucose)
- A1C ≥6.5% (using a standardized assay)
- Random plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL with classic symptoms of hyperglycemia

Prediabetes (impaired fasting glucose or impaired glucose tolerance):
- Fasting glucose: 100–125 mg/dL
- 2-hour OGTT: 140–199 mg/dL
- A1C: 5.7–6.4%

GLYCEMIC TARGETS
For most non-pregnant adults with type 2 diabetes:
- A1C target: < 7.0%
- Preprandial glucose: 80–130 mg/dL
- Peak postprandial glucose (1–2 h after meal): < 180 mg/dL

Less stringent targets (A1C < 8.0%) may be appropriate for:
- Limited life expectancy
- Extensive comorbidities
- High hypoglycemia risk
- Long duration of diabetes with difficulty achieving target

More stringent targets (A1C < 6.5%) may be considered in younger patients
with short disease duration and no cardiovascular disease, if achievable without
significant hypoglycemia or polypharmacy.

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS
Lifestyle intervention is the cornerstone of type 2 diabetes management.

1. Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT):
   - No single optimal macronutrient distribution; individualize to patient
   - Reduce processed carbohydrates; prefer low-glycemic index foods
   - Caloric restriction to achieve weight loss in overweight/obese patients
   - Mediterranean or DASH diet patterns show benefit
   - Referral to a registered dietitian recommended

2. Physical Activity:
   - ≥150 minutes/week of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise
   - Resistance training ≥2 days/week
   - Reduce prolonged sedentary time; break up sitting every 30 minutes
   - Pre-exercise screening for cardiovascular disease and neuropathy

3. Weight Management:
   - Even modest weight loss (5–10% of body weight) significantly improves glycemia
   - Goal: BMI < 25; in Asians < 23 kg/m²
   - Consider referral for bariatric surgery in BMI ≥35 (or ≥30 in some guidelines)

4. Smoking Cessation:
   - Smoking worsens insulin resistance and cardiovascular risk
   - Offer counseling and pharmacotherapy (varenicline, bupropion, NRT)

5. Alcohol:
   - Moderate use: ≤1 drink/day (women), ≤2 drinks/day (men)
   - Monitor for hypoglycemia especially with insulin or sulfonylureas

PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT
First-line: Metformin (if tolerated and not contraindicated)
- Reduces hepatic glucose production
- Weight-neutral or modest weight loss
- Contraindicated in severe renal impairment (eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²)
- Hold before contrast procedures

Second-line agents (choice driven by comorbidities):

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (e.g., semaglutide, liraglutide, dulaglutide):
- Preferred for patients with established ASCVD or high CV risk
- Significant weight loss benefit
- Once weekly or daily injectable; oral semaglutide available

SGLT-2 Inhibitors (e.g., empagliflozin, dapagliflozin, canagliflozin):
- Preferred in patients with HFrEF or CKD
- Reduces hospitalization for heart failure
- Risk of genital mycotic infections and DKA (especially off-label use)

DPP-4 Inhibitors (e.g., sitagliptin, saxagliptin):
- Weight neutral; well-tolerated
- Saxagliptin associated with increased HF hospitalization

Sulfonylureas (e.g., glipizide, glyburide, glimepiride):
- Inexpensive; effective
- Risk of hypoglycemia and weight gain

Insulin therapy:
- Used when oral agents are insufficient
- Basal insulin (long-acting) often added first
- Prandial insulin added for post-meal hyperglycemia
- A1C reduction: 1.5–3.5%

CARDIOVASCULAR RISK REDUCTION
- Blood pressure target: < 130/80 mmHg
- LDL-C target: < 70 mg/dL for high-risk patients (established ASCVD)
- Statin therapy: moderate-to-high intensity recommended for most adults with T2DM aged 40–75
- Aspirin (75–162 mg/day): for secondary prevention only; not recommended for primary prevention

SCREENING FOR COMPLICATIONS
At diagnosis, then annually:
- Diabetic kidney disease: urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio + eGFR
- Diabetic retinopathy: dilated fundus exam
- Diabetic neuropathy: foot exam with monofilament, vibration, ankle reflexes
- Cardiovascular disease: BP, lipids, ECG if symptomatic

HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT
Mild-moderate: 15g fast-acting carbohydrates (glucose tablets, juice) — "rule of 15"
Wait 15 minutes; recheck; repeat if BG < 70 mg/dL
Severe (cannot self-treat): glucagon kit (nasal or injection); call emergency services
Adjust medications to prevent recurrence
