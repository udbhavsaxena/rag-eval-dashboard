CARDIOVASCULAR DISEASE PREVENTION GUIDELINES
Clinical Practice Summary (Demo Document — Not Medical Advice)

PRIMARY PREVENTION OVERVIEW
Primary prevention refers to interventions to prevent a first cardiovascular event
in individuals without established CVD. Risk assessment is the foundation.

CARDIOVASCULAR RISK ASSESSMENT
10-Year ASCVD Risk Calculation (Pooled Cohort Equations):
- Inputs: age, sex, race, total cholesterol, HDL-C, systolic BP, BP treatment status,
  diabetes status, smoking status
- High risk: ≥10% 10-year ASCVD risk
- Intermediate risk: 7.5–<10%
- Borderline risk: 5–<7.5%
- Low risk: <5%

Risk-Enhancing Factors (to consider when risk is borderline/intermediate):
- Family history of premature ASCVD (males <55, females <65)
- LDL-C ≥160 mg/dL
- Metabolic syndrome
- CKD
- Inflammatory conditions (RA, psoriasis, HIV)
- Premature menopause or history of preeclampsia
- South Asian ancestry
- High-sensitivity CRP ≥2 mg/L
- Ankle-brachial index < 0.9
- Coronary artery calcium (CAC) score > 0

STATIN THERAPY
Indications for statin therapy:
1. Clinical ASCVD (secondary prevention): high-intensity statin regardless of LDL
2. LDL ≥190 mg/dL: high-intensity statin
3. Diabetes (age 40–75): moderate-intensity statin; high-intensity if ≥20% 10-yr risk
4. Primary prevention (age 40–75, LDL 70–189, ≥7.5% 10-yr risk): moderate-to-high statin

Statin intensity:
- High-intensity: ≥50% LDL reduction
  (atorvastatin 40–80 mg, rosuvastatin 20–40 mg)
- Moderate-intensity: 30–49% LDL reduction
  (atorvastatin 10–20 mg, simvastatin 20–40 mg, pravastatin 40–80 mg)
- Low-intensity: <30% LDL reduction

LDL Targets (for statin-treated patients):
- Secondary prevention (very high risk): LDL < 55 mg/dL (ESC) or < 70 mg/dL (ACC/AHA)
- Primary prevention (high risk): < 70 mg/dL
- Primary prevention (moderate risk): < 100 mg/dL

Non-statin lipid-lowering:
- Ezetimibe: add to statin if LDL not at goal (reduces LDL ~18–20%)
- PCSK9 inhibitors (evolocumab, alirocumab): for very high-risk patients
  not at LDL goal on maximally tolerated statin + ezetimibe
- Bempedoic acid: for statin-intolerant patients

ANTIPLATELET THERAPY
Aspirin for primary prevention:
- Low-dose aspirin (81 mg/day) is NOT routinely recommended for primary prevention
  in most adults due to increased bleeding risk outweighing benefit
- May be considered in selected patients aged 40–70 at high ASCVD risk and low bleed risk
  (USPSTF 2022 guideline)
- Aspirin for secondary prevention: 81 mg/day indefinitely after MI or stroke

Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT):
- After ACS: aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (ticagrelor or prasugrel preferred over clopidogrel)
  for 12 months, then long-term aspirin
- After elective PCI with stent: 6 months DAPT minimum; may extend in high ischemic risk

BLOOD PRESSURE IN CVD PREVENTION
- Target < 130/80 mmHg for adults with CVD or high 10-year ASCVD risk ≥10%
- Even modest reductions in SBP significantly reduce CVD events
- Treat with lifestyle first; add medications if lifestyle insufficient after 3 months

LIFESTYLE INTERVENTIONS FOR CVD PREVENTION
1. Smoking cessation: single most important modifiable risk factor
   - Pharmacotherapy: varenicline (most effective), bupropion, NRT
   - Reduces CVD risk substantially within 1–2 years

2. Diet:
   - Mediterranean or DASH dietary pattern
   - Reduce saturated fat; replace with unsaturated fats (olive oil, nuts)
   - Increase omega-3 fatty acids (fish 2×/week or supplements in high-risk)
   - Reduce processed food and added sugars

3. Physical Activity:
   - 150 min/week moderate-intensity or 75 min/week vigorous aerobic activity
   - Muscle-strengthening activities ≥2 days/week
   - Reduces CVD risk by 30–35%

4. Weight Management:
   - BMI 18.5–24.9 kg/m²; waist circumference: <102 cm (men), <88 cm (women)
   - Even 5–10% weight reduction improves CVD risk factors

5. Alcohol:
   - No alcohol is the safest level for cardiovascular health (WHO 2022)
   - If drinking: limit to ≤1 standard drink/day

METABOLIC SYNDROME
Diagnosis requires ≥3 of 5 criteria:
1. Waist circumference ≥102 cm (men) or ≥88 cm (women)
2. Fasting triglycerides ≥150 mg/dL
3. HDL-C < 40 mg/dL (men) or < 50 mg/dL (women)
4. Blood pressure ≥130/85 mmHg (or on antihypertensive treatment)
5. Fasting glucose ≥100 mg/dL (or on glucose-lowering treatment)

Treatment: primarily lifestyle modification; address individual components

SCREENING AND MONITORING
- Lipid panel: adults aged 20+ every 4–6 years; more frequently if high risk
- Fasting glucose and A1C: every 3 years if at risk for diabetes
- Blood pressure: every year if normal; more frequently if elevated
- 10-year ASCVD risk: recalculate every 4–6 years
